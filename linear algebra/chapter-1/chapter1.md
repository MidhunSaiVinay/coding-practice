# Vectors Part 1

- **Dimensionality:**

 The number of numbers in the vector.
 
  Dimensionality is often indicated using a fancy-looking ℝN, where the ℝ indicates
 real-valued numbers (cf. ℂ for complex-valued numbers) and the N indicates the
 dimensionality. For example, a vector with two elements is said to be a member of ℝ2.
 That special ℝ character is made using latex code, but you can also write R2, R2, or R^2.

- **Orientation:**

 Whether the vector is in column orientation (standing up tall) or row orientation
 (laying flat and wide)

- **Transpose**
converting row vector to column and column to row

$$
m_{i,j}$ = m_{j,i}
$$

- **Vector Magnitude**
Length or norm of vector


$$
\| v \| = \sum_{i=1}^{n} v_i^2
$$

- **Vector Dot Product**

$$
\delta = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i
$$
